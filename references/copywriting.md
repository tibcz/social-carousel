# Carousel Copywriting Playbook

The carousel format is a slideshow the reader controls. Every swipe is a decision to keep reading. Write every slide to earn the next swipe.

## The narrative arc

Map content onto this skeleton, then cut until nothing is removable:

| # | Role | Job | Slide type in templates |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Hook** | Stop the scroll. ≤ 12 words. No setup, no logo-only slides. | `slide--cover` |
| 2 | **Stakes / promise** | Why this matters to *them*, what they'll get by the last slide. | `slide--context` |
| 3–8 | **Beats** | One idea each. Number them — numbered slides get completed. | `slide--point`, `slide--body`, `slide--quote`, `slide--list` |
| 9 | **Recap** | The whole argument on one slide. This is the screenshot people share. | `slide--recap` |
| 10 | **CTA** | One ask. Exactly one. | `slide--cta` |

Rhythm rule: vary slide types. Two identical layouts in a row is fine; three is a wall. Drop a quote or big-number slide in the middle of long lists.

## Hook formulas (slide 1)

Pick the one that fits the content — don't force a formula onto a weak premise:

1. **Number + outcome** — "7 prompts that replaced my $400/mo tools"
2. **Contrarian** — "Code reviews are making your team slower"
3. **Costly mistake** — "This one settings toggle cost us 40% of our reach"
4. **Earned secret** — "What 200 failed launches taught me about pricing"
5. **Direct callout** — "If you write docs, stop doing this"
6. **Before/after tease** — "From 0 to 12k followers with one format change"
7. **Named enemy** — "AI slop is killing your engagement. Here's the fix"

Hook quality bar: would a stranger screenshot slide 1 alone and send it to a friend? If not, rewrite it.

## Body slide rules

- **≤ 40 words per slide.** Headline ≤ 8 words; supporting text ≤ 30 words.
- Concrete beats abstract: "cut review time from 2 days to 20 minutes", never "improve efficiency".
- Each slide's last line should create a small open loop where natural ("…but the third one is counterintuitive").
- Active voice, second person. The reader is "you", not "users" or "one".
- Numbers as digits ("7", not "seven"); they scan faster in big type.

### Anti-slop pass (mandatory)

Carousel copy gets read aloud in people's heads. Strip the AI tells before delivering:

- No "it's not just X, it's Y" constructions
- No "delve", "unlock", "elevate", "game-changer", "in today's fast-paced world"
- No triple parallel adjectives ("faster, smarter, better")
- Max one em-dash per deck
- No hedging ("can potentially help") — commit or cut the claim

## Recap slide

Restate the beats as a tight numbered list, ≤ 6 words per line. Title it with the promise, not "Summary" (e.g. "The 5-step cold-open formula"). This slide gets shared standalone — it must work with zero context.

## CTA patterns (slide N)

One ask only. Stacked asks ("like, follow, comment AND share") convert worse than one.

- **Follow** — "Follow @handle for [specific topic] every week" (specificity is the persuasion)
- **Comment trigger** — "Which one are you trying first? Tell me below" (comments outrank likes in every feed algorithm)
- **Save** — "Save this for your next [task]" (saves are the strongest signal on Instagram)
- **Lead magnet** — "Comment 'GUIDE' and I'll send the full checklist" (LinkedIn/IG DM automation pattern)

Match the CTA to the platform: Save → Instagram, Comment → LinkedIn, Follow → X/TikTok.

## Caption formula (deliver with every carousel)

```
[Hook line — may repeat slide 1, that's fine]

[2–4 short lines: the context or story the slides don't tell]

[CTA mirroring the last slide]

[3–5 hashtags]
```

- **LinkedIn:** first 2 lines show before "…see more" — front-load. 3 hashtags max, niche over broad. No links in the post body (put links in comments).
- **Instagram:** caption is secondary to slides; 5 hashtags, mix one broad + four niche. CTA "Save this".
- **X:** the tweet text IS the hook; one line + the images. No hashtags.
- **TikTok:** short caption + 3–4 hashtags; the text-to-speech crowd reads captions aloud, keep it clean.
